# Whiskly Baker Recruitment Strategy

## Goal

8 new bakers in 4 to 6 weeks. Combined with current 2 bakers = 10 to 12, enough to open the customer side.

## Channel mix and volume math

Recommended effort split: **30% warm, 70% cold.**

- Warm = referrals, your network, friends-of-friends
- Cold = Instagram DMs, Facebook groups, wedding planner introductions

Why this split: warm converts higher (20 to 30%) but the pool is small and runs dry fast. Cold has lower conversion (1 to 3%) but is unlimited and tells you whether the pitch actually works on people who don't already know you.

**Volume targets for the full 4-week sprint:**

| Channel | Outreach volume | Expected conversion | Expected bakers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Warm referrals + network | 20 attempts | ~25% | 5 bakers |
| Cold Instagram DMs | 200 attempts | ~2% | 4 bakers |
| Facebook group inbound | 5 to 10 leads/month | ~30% (already interested) | 2 to 3 bakers |
| **Total expected** |  |  | **11 to 12 bakers** |

That's 50 outreach attempts per week. Roughly 10 a weekday. Doable alongside JFP work if you batch.

## Channel 2: Instagram DMs (Cold)

**Goal:** 200 DMs in 4 weeks = 50/week = 10 per weekday.

### Finding bakers

Search these hashtags and filter for active DMV-area accounts:

- #dmvbaker, #dmvcakes, #dmvfoodie
- #marylandbaker, #marylandcakes, #marylandcustomcakes, #marylandhomebaker
- #dccakes, #washingtondccakes, #dcbaker
- #virginiacakes, #northernvirginiabaker, #novacakes
- #cottagebaker, #cottagebakerlife
- Cross-search: location-based foodie hashtags + "cake" or "dessert"

**Qualify for outreach:**
- Posted within last 30 days (account is active)
- Real engagement on recent posts (5+ comments from non-bots)
- Bio includes signals of taking orders: "DM to order," "custom orders," cottage food law disclaimer, pickup location
- 500 to 10,000 followers ideal range
- Avoid: 50K+ followers (won't bother), under 200 followers (likely hobby not business), accounts that haven't posted in 60+ days

Build a spreadsheet of 50 qualifying bakers before sending any DMs. Batch the research, batch the outreach.

### Cold DM script (first touch)

> Hey [Name], saw your work and your [specific item, e.g., "watercolor buttercream technique"] is gorgeous. I'm a baker too, and I built a platform for home bakers in MD/DC/VA called Whiskly to handle everything that's not the actual baking: bookings, deposits, customer communication, delivery details, all in one place.
>
> We're recruiting our first 50 founding bakers right now and I'd love to send you details if you're interested. Free to join, no monthly fee, no commission. Founding Baker status is permanent.
>
> Want me to send the link?

**Critical elements:**
- "I'm a baker too" is your strongest differentiator. Always lead with it.
- Always reference something specific from their actual work. Generic DMs get ignored 95% of the time.
- End with a low-commitment ask ("send the link?") not a high-commitment one ("get on a call?"). Lower friction = higher reply rate.

### If they reply yes:

> Great! Here's the page: whiskly.co/for-bakers
>
> The Founding Baker offer is the part to focus on: a permanent badge, priority placement for the first 12 months, a 30-day free trial, and locked-in $14/month (or $99/year) pricing and 7% commission for three years.
>
> Application takes about 10 minutes: whiskly.co/join
>
> Happy to answer questions before you apply. What's on your mind?

### Follow-up script (5 days no response)

> Hey [Name], following up in case my last DM got buried. Still [N] Founding Baker spots open if you want to grab one. Here's the page: whiskly.co/for-bakers

After two no-responses, move on. Don't push.

## Channel 3: Facebook Groups (Mixed)

**Goal:** Establish presence in 5 groups, generate 5 to 10 inbound leads per month.

### Groups to join

Request access to:
- "Maryland Cottage Food Law" or your state's equivalent
- "DMV Home Bakers" or regional baker groups
- "Cottage Food Bakers of America" or similar national
- Local county-level small business support groups
- Wedding industry vendor groups in DMV (planners, photographers, florists often share with bakers)

### Strategy: Be useful first, recruit second

Don't post a recruitment ad your first day. You'll get banned or ignored.

For 2 to 4 weeks, build credibility:
- Comment helpfully on other people's posts (deposit policies, pricing questions, delivery logistics)
- Post your own value content occasionally:
  - "How I structure custom cake deposits to avoid no-shows"
  - "What I learned from 6 months of taking orders through Instagram"
  - "Maryland cottage food law update for [topic]"
- Build name recognition. Members start to know you as the helpful baker.

After credibility is established, post one introduction:

### Group introduction post (after 2 to 4 weeks of presence)

> Hi all, I'm Lxy. I've been a home baker in MD and got tired of running my entire business through Instagram DMs and Venmo. So I built a platform called Whiskly for home bakers like us. Structured booking, deposits, customer communication, all in one place.
>
> We're in early access and recruiting our first 50 founding bakers right now. If anyone wants to take a look or has questions about the model, comment or DM me. Happy to share more.
>
> [Link to whiskly.co/for-bakers]

Frame as a community share, not a promotion. Check group rules first. Some groups require admin approval for promotional posts.

When someone DMs interest, use the Instagram "yes reply" script above.

## Channel 4: Channels you might not have considered

You said you weren't sure which channels you have access to. Here's what you might be missing:

### LinkedIn (warm, high-leverage)

Your federal IT and JFP professional network includes event planners, wedding industry contacts, HR people doing corporate event planning, government office event coordinators. Any of those people personally know bakers or hire them for events. Soft ask:

> Hi [Name], random ask: do you know any home bakers in the DMV area who do custom orders? I'm building a platform for cottage bakers and looking for our first 50 founding members. Happy to share details if you have someone in mind.

This is one DM that could yield 3 to 5 baker introductions per response.

### Wedding planners (high-leverage cold)

Planners know bakers. Cold-message DMV wedding planners on Instagram or LinkedIn:

> Hi [Name], I'm building a platform for home bakers in the DMV area to streamline custom orders. Looking for our first 50 founding bakers. Do you know any independent bakers you'd recommend I reach out to? Happy to send a fee-free founding spot their way if it's a fit.

One planner reply often yields 5 to 10 baker introductions. Highest leverage outreach you can do.

### Bridal show vendor lists

Most DMV bridal shows publish their vendor lists with contact info. Bakers/dessert vendors at recent shows are by definition active in the wedding market and willing to invest in marketing. Cold email works better for this segment than cottage bakers in general.

Search: "Maryland bridal show vendors 2025," "DC wedding expo vendors 2025."

### Maryland Cottage Food Law association

Search whether your state has an organized cottage food association. They often have member directories or newsletters where you can place a note about Whiskly. Direct line to bakers who are already legally compliant and serious about their business.

### TikTok

You didn't list it but cottage baker content is huge there. DM culture is less established than Instagram, but commenting on baker videos and starting conversations works. Lower priority than IG but worth 15 minutes of exploration.

## Founding Baker urgency framing

The 10-baker cap is your single strongest asset. Use it consistently:

- "42 Founding Baker spots left"
- "We're recruiting our first 50. Once we hit 50, the offer closes permanently."
- NOT "founding spots are limited" (vague)

As you sign bakers, update the public counter on /for-bakers AND in every DM. Visible scarcity drives action. Once you hit 5/10, urgency rises sharply for the remaining outreach.

## When to stop and reassess

After 2 weeks, evaluate:

| Metric | Threshold | If below: |
|---|---|---|
| Cold DM response rate | < 5% | Revise the script (more specific personalization, different opener) |
| Response-to-application conversion | < 10% | The 7-step application is too long. Shorten it. |
| Warm referrals per existing baker | < 1 | Your current 2 bakers might not feel ownership of Whiskly. Worth a check-in conversation. |

After 4 weeks, if you have fewer than 4 new bakers, the channel mix isn't working. Pivot to in-person:
- Local farmers markets (talk to dessert vendors directly)
- DMV bridal shows (get a vendor table or just walk and talk)
- Bake sales and church events (cottage bakers often sell here)

In-person converts at 30 to 50% but doesn't scale. Use as the bridge to launch if digital channels stall.
